This is the monthly management report for the Scottish Robotic-Assisted Surgery Audit (SRASA). SRASA is being conducted by the Scottish National Audit Programme (SNAP) at Public Health Scotland (PHS) on behalf of the Scottish Government, to assess and monitor the usage of robotic-assisted intracavity surgery across NHSScotland.
The audit aims to monitor the utilisation of Da Vinci robotic systems in NHSScotland, and will go on to investigate equity of access to robotic surgery and patient safety in due course. Currently, this report shows total utilisation, broken down at surgical specialty level, and procedure level.
This report is based on analysis of the Scottish Morbidity Records – General Acute Inpatient and Day Case (SMR01). An SMR01 record is populated based on information collated from patient notes – including notes from surgical and post-surgical care as well as discharge letters etc. – interpreted by a specialist clinical coder to determine the crucial information (including diagnoses and procedures) relating to a patient’s episode of care. As such, the completeness of note-taking throughout a patient’s care journey is essential to the subsequent quality of the dataset, to minimise the possibility of mis-interpretation by coders.
SRASA also has access to the data collected by Intuitive at the point of use of each Da Vinci robotic system, which we can use as a benchmark to measure any shortfall in identifying RAS surgeries from SMR01. See the ‘Data Quality’ tab for visualisation of this benchmarking. Due to the lack of patient-identifiable information in this data collection, however, it is not suitable as the main source of data for the audit, and cannot be definitively linked to patient records in SMR01.
We would therefore welcome any feedback on the data presented for your site. Please get in touch with the SRASA team at phs.srasa@phs.scot. We are aware that we are not currently capturing all robotic surgery activity in NHSScotland, and this is thought to be mainly due to coding inaccuracies in SMR01. Your engagement is vital to help us track down the common sources of these inaccuracies and take steps to minimise them wherever possible.
